BIOGRAPHY

From Early Pencil Sketches to Mastery of Paint

Beata Robak discovered her passion for art as early as kindergarten, when drawing became her first form of self-expression. In primary school, it was her art teacher who recognized her unique potential and encouraged her to further develop her artistic skills.

For a long time, Beata avoided painting with paints, convinced that she could not master them. Instead, she focused mainly on pencil portraits, later expanding her practice to include colored pencils and soft pastels. Her true fascination with acrylics and oil paints emerged much later, inspired by her growing passion for collecting art reproductions, which gradually motivated her to give painting another chance.

Alongside her artistic development, she cultivated a deep interest in history from an early age. Over time, she merged this passion with art—studying history, particularly ancient and medieval periods, alongside art history and painting itself.
